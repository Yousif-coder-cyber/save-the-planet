However, climate change, deforestation, and poor land management have led to severe ecological challenges.

Climate Change Effects

Climate change is causing extreme temperatures, prolonged droughts, and desertification. The Tigris and Euphrates rivers are shrinking due to upstream damming, reducing water availability for agriculture and daily life. The loss of wetlands threatens biodiversity, while air and water pollution worsen public health.

Geographical Zones and Environmental Challenges

  • Desert and Semi-Arid Zones: Cover large parts of Iraq, experiencing extreme heat, water shortages, and minimal vegetation.
  • Fertile Crescent: Once a thriving agricultural hub, now facing soil degradation and water scarcity.
  • Mountainous North: A biodiversity hotspot, but deforestation and human encroachment threaten native species.
  • Marshlands: Home to rich biodiversity, but drying due to climate change and human activities.

How to Help

  • Plant more trees and drought-resistant vegetation to restore ecosystems.
  • Reduce water consumption and promote efficient irrigation methods.
  • Support wetland restoration and prevent illegal land reclamation.
  • Advocate for policies that promote sustainable land and water management.
  • Encourage recycling and reduce plastic waste to limit pollution.

Plants to Help Restore Balance

Mesquite Tree

Thrives in arid environments, prevents desertification, and improves soil fertility.

Tamarisk Tree

Absorbs excess salt from the soil, combats desert spread, and provides windbreaks.

Date Palm

Provides shade, stabilizes soil, and supports agriculture in dry regions.

Moringa Tree

Fast-growing, purifies water, restores soil, and provides nutrition.

Eucalyptus Tree

Absorbs pollutants, improves air quality, and prevents soil erosion.

Acacia Tree

Drought-resistant tree that enriches soil with nitrogen and prevents land degradation.

Sophora Tree

Survives extreme heat, improves soil health, and helps reforest dry areas.
